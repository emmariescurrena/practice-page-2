Focus ability

What is it?

Focus ability refers to the capacity of be able to channel all your energy and attention on a single task, stimulus, etc.

Why is important?

The human brain reachs his maximum processing potential, among other things, by doing one thing at the time. Suppose that a brain has 100 of power. Being able to focus secures you to have in hands at least 90 of that power. But if your attention is fractionated, suppose, in 2 tasks, the power of the tasks added doesn't reach the 70. Summarizing, we have more power in hands and we also optimize our energy, because both scenarios, supposing that you are trying to focus in the second case, spend the same energy.

How to improve it

First of all: Measure your focus

Knowing your capacities to stay focus gives you a reference to track the progress in this journey. This can be done by starting to work in something that needs you to stay focus to understand and doesn't bore you. Start a cronometer and stop it when try to notice you are losing focus. This is our reference. If you are not secure of the fairness of this test, test you again with the same task and with some others to have more references to see and reveal if your lack of focus is with a particular task.

Silence your phone

For some people this advice is the end of the world. This device is the best focus-breaker existent. If you think that someone can call you for an emergency, first think which is gonna call you before calling someone more. And if you are waiting a call, silence only the notifications. For those who can't stop to look at the phone inclusive being silenced, is recommended to move the phone to another room or give it to another person. For this people, who wander about other things while trying focusing, you can try to have a notebook to write that things to work them later.

Care about your body's health

There is a ton of research about the correlation between your physical and your mental health. The best thing for the brain is just move. If you don't trust me, try it for yourself. And I'm not talking only about going to the gym or running. Playing a sport, walking, swimming are included. the point is to, again, move. You're gonna feel it.

Work with fixed time chunks and rest

Scheduling your time to work excempts you from know when to stop working, giving you a plus of focus and knowing when to rest. For example, if your focus lasts 25 mins, work 25 min and rest for, like, 5. After some chunks, in this case let say 4, take a longer break, like 20 minutes. The rest between chunks and the longer break are proportional to the duration of the chunks. The important thing is to find work chunks according to your focus strength.
